The Effects of Pollution on Beaches

Please be mindful of where you discard your refuse.

Never litter! Irresponsible disposal contaminates and pollutes.

Your litter get dispersed and make their way into waterways, beaches and seas.

Litter also comes back as carcinogens through the water supply and food chain.

Litter kill many forms of life.

Impacts of Mismanaged Trash

1.1. Habitats Impacts: Habitat alteration is caused by trash and debris in rivers and oceanic convergence zones, on beaches, and near the bottom of rivers and oceans.

1.2. Chemical Impacts: Plastic particles once released to the environment can be eaten by some marine species, for example, in a study by Ryan et al (1988), great shearwaters (Puffinus gravis), a seabird known to ingest plastic, had concentrations of plastic in fat tissues corresponding to the amounts of plastic found in their stomachs.

1.3. Biological Impacts: It has been estimated that plastic marine debris adversely affects at least 267 species globally, including 86% of sea turtles, 44% of seabirds, and 43% of marine mammals.

1.4. Humans Impacts: Plastic aquatic debris can directly interfere with navigation, impede commercial and recreational fishing, threaten health and safety, and reduce tourism.

What We Can Do

  • Avoid polluting with domestic or street garbage
  • Do not feed the birds or wildlife
  • Collect your pet's waste
  • Avoid contamination of boats
  • Carry a reusable water bottle
  • Participating in picking up trash and other waste on beach
