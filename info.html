What is a Beach?

What is a beach? A beach is the shore of a body of water that has sand, pebbles or rocks. When we think of a beach, most imagine a wide oceanic beach with sand, aquatic plants and dunes in the background, where the waves break, the wind blows and seagulls fly overhead.


Why are beaches important?

The beaches offer, any recreational opportunities for millions of people like enjoy sailing, fishing, swimming, walking, collecting objects, watching birds, playing and sunbathing, among other activities. The beaches serve as protection for residents who live near the sea, as they act as a defense against strong winds and waves caused by storms or rough seas. Beaches also have an important role in the economy. Enjoying ocean beaches is one of the outdoor activities based on nature that is growing the most in the United States.
